## Description
An experience organised around a moment of sudden insight or revelation that fundamentally shifted your understanding of yourself or the world. This structure privileges the discrete transformative moment where clarity emerged, suggesting that meaning crystallizes in specific instances rather than gradual processes.

## Prompts
1. What were you thinking, feeling, or doing in the moments leading up to your realization?
2. Describe the moment of insight itself—what suddenly became clear to you?
3. What triggered or sparked this realization? Was it something you saw, heard, felt, or understood?
4. How did your understanding of the situation change before and after this moment?
5. What has this revelation meant for how you see yourself or your life since then?

## Section Structure
Each section should feel self-contained — landing somewhere emotionally — while making the next feel necessary. The structure orbits a single moment of clarity, moving toward it and then away.

- **Section 1 — The Lead-Up**: The texture of the ordinary before the realisation — what the protagonist was doing, feeling, not yet understanding. The conditions that made the moment possible without yet revealing it. Ends at the threshold of the insight, not yet there.
- **Section 2 — The Moment**: The realisation itself, rendered with full attention — what suddenly became clear, what triggered it, how the world shifted in the instant. Short, precise, charged. Ends in the immediate aftermath: the protagonist still blinking in the new light.
- **Section 3 — The Aftermath**: How the epiphany has lived in the protagonist since — what it changed, what it made possible, how they see themselves or the world differently now. Closes with a sense of before and after held together: the person who didn't know, and the person who does.
