## Description
An experience as moral and psychological development from naivety toward maturity, emphasizing the formation of identity and values. This structure highlights how the experience—whether positive or negative—contributed to becoming a more complete, self-aware person with clearer principles.

## Prompts
1. Who were you before this experience? What did you believe about yourself or the world?
2. What aspects of this experience challenged or complicated your previous understanding?
3. What internal conflict or tension did you grapple with during this experience?
4. What did you come to understand or realize that you hadn't before?
5. How did this experience shape who you are now—your values, beliefs, or sense of self?

## Section Structure
Each section should feel self-contained — landing somewhere emotionally — while making the next feel necessary.

- **Section 1 — Before**: Who the protagonist was before this experience. Their assumptions about themselves and the world — rendered with clarity and some tenderness, not condescension. Ends at the moment something begins to challenge that understanding.
- **Section 2 — The Conflict**: The internal struggle: the old self and the new reality in tension. What the experience demanded, what the protagonist resisted, what began to crack. Ends at the moment of maximum uncertainty — identity in flux, no resolution yet.
- **Section 3 — After**: Who they became. Not a sudden transformation but a gradual settling — values clarified, beliefs revised, a self more fully inhabited. Closes with a quiet sense of arrival at a more honest version of themselves.
