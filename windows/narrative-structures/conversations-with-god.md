## Description
An experience that you must account for and justify to a transcendent witness or ultimate authority. This structure prompts reflection on the moral weight and broader significance of your choices, as if explaining them to an all-seeing judge who demands you articulate your values and rationale.

## Prompts
1. If you had to justify your actions in this experience to a higher power, what would you say you were trying to accomplish or protect?
2. What values or principles guided your choices, even if the outcome was uncertain or imperfect?
3. What would you need to explain or defend about your decisions? What might be questioned or judged?
4. Looking at this experience from the perspective of ultimate accountability, what matters most about how you acted?
5. If this higher power could see into your heart, what would they understand about why you did what you did?

## Section Structure
Each section should feel self-contained — landing somewhere emotionally — while making the next feel necessary. The framing is an implied dialogue: the protagonist speaks; the higher power listens and weighs.

- **Section 1 — The Accounting**: The protagonist states what they were trying to accomplish or protect. The values that guided them. Rendered not as justification but as honest testimony. Ends with the sense that something harder is still to be faced.
- **Section 2 — The Defence**: What must be explained or defended — the decisions that could be questioned or judged. The protagonist neither collapses nor deflects. Ends with the hardest thing said aloud, hanging in the silence.
- **Section 3 — What Is Seen**: What the higher power would understand about the heart beneath the choices — the fear, the love, the intention that no one else could fully see. Closes not with verdict but with recognition: to be truly known and not condemned.
