## Description
An experience as a quest where you venture from the familiar into challenge, undergo transformation, and emerge changed with newfound wisdom or capability. The emphasis is on personal growth through adversity, with clear phases of departure, trials, and return that give meaning to the difficulty you faced.

## Prompts
1. What makes you you? Think about your identity, personality, core values.
2. What change of setting or novel experience prompted your journey to become who you are today?
3. What overall goal were you striving for that led to who you are today?
4. What challenges or obstacles, such as a nemesis/rival or negative event, stood in the way of your journey?
5. In what ways has your journey left an impact—on yourself or those around you?

## Section Structure
Each section should feel self-contained — landing somewhere emotionally — while making the next feel necessary.

- **Section 1 — Departure**: The ordinary world and what disturbed it. Who the protagonist was before the journey began, and the moment that called them away from the familiar. Ends on the threshold — the point of no return just ahead.
- **Section 2 — Trials**: The struggle toward the goal. The obstacles, the adversaries, the moments of doubt. Something is lost or transformed here. Ends at the deepest point of the ordeal — the outcome still uncertain.
- **Section 3 — Return**: The emergence. What was gained, what was left behind, how the world looks different now. The protagonist is changed; the story closes with a sense of hard-won arrival.
