## Description
An experience as a fall from grace followed by recovery and renewal, where mistakes or suffering become the foundation for deeper understanding and positive change. The intervention emphasizes how the setback wasn't an endpoint but a catalyst for redemption, growth, or finding new purpose.

## Prompts
1. What was lost, damaged, or diminished in this experience? What did you lose or what went wrong?
2. What was your lowest point—the moment when things felt most broken or hopeless?
3. What shifted that allowed you to begin moving forward or finding meaning in what happened?
4. How did you rebuild, recover, or find a new path after this experience?
5. In what ways are you stronger, wiser, or more capable now because of what you went through?

## Section Structure
Each section should feel self-contained — landing somewhere emotionally — while making the next feel necessary.

- **Section 1 — The Fall**: What was lost, broken, or destroyed. The full weight of the damage. Does not soften or explain — just renders the descent clearly. Ends at the edge of the lowest point, not yet inside it.
- **Section 2 — The Lowest Point**: The moment things felt most hopeless — and the first, fragile shift. Something changes: a realisation, a decision, an unexpected kindness. The turn is small but real. Ends with the protagonist facing forward for the first time.
- **Section 3 — Rebuilding**: The slow, uneven work of recovery. Not a triumphant return but a genuine one — what was rebuilt, what couldn't be, and what was found that wasn't there before. Closes with a sense of hard-won ground.
