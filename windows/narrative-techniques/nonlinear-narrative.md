## Description
Breaking chronological order to juxtapose distant moments, start in the middle of the action, or rearrange causality to reveal connections invisible in linear time. This technique allows you to see how later events reinterpret earlier ones, or how seemingly unrelated moments form meaningful patterns when reordered.

## Prompts
1. Start in the middle: What was the moment of highest tension, conflict, or intensity in this experience?
2. Now step back—what earlier moment or decision set this intense moment in motion? What planted the seed?
3. Looking forward from that peak moment, what happened next that surprised you or changed the direction of events?
4. If you had to identify a moment that only made sense after everything else happened—something that seemed insignificant at the time but now feels crucial—what would it be?
5. How do these moments connect to each other in ways that a simple beginning-to-end telling would miss?

## Section Structure
Each section should feel self-contained — landing somewhere emotionally — while making the next feel necessary. Chronology is deliberately broken; each section should feel like it belongs at a different point in the story's timeline.

- **Section 1 — The Peak**: Begin in the middle of the highest tension or intensity — no setup, no explanation. The reader is dropped into the moment. Ends mid-action, mid-consequence, with the cause still unknown.
- **Section 2 — The Seed**: Step back to an earlier moment — the decision, event, or quality that set the peak in motion. Something that seemed small or unrelated at the time. Now, with the peak in mind, its significance is unmistakable. Ends with the thread leading forward again.
- **Section 3 — The Revelation**: Move to what came after — and to the moment that only made sense once everything else had happened. A detail, a realisation, a connection. Closes with the full shape of the story visible at last: not despite the broken order, but because of it.
